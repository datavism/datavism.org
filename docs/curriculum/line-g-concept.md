# LINE G — GHOST / Foundation

**The Initiation Track of the Evidence Engine**

- **Status:** Draft v0.2 (canon — authored by Frank, 2026-06-20)
- **Role:** Foundation / Initiation / Method Line
- **Mentor:** GHOST
- **Line Motto:** Ask better. Command cleaner. Verify harder.
- **Core Question:** How do I turn a vague suspicion into a verifiable investigation?
- **Final Artifact:** Eval Report / First Case File
- **Map Function:** Entry line. Every Datavist starts here.

---

## 1. What LINE G Is

LINE G is the operating system of DATAVISM.

It is not a beginner course in the usual sense. It does not begin with syntax.
It does not begin with tools. It does not begin with a dashboard. It begins with
a feeling:

> Something is happening to me, around me, or through me — but I cannot yet prove it.

LINE G teaches the learner how to transform that feeling into an investigation.
The progression is:

**Suspicion → Question → Command → Intake → Verification → Evaluation → Case File**

LINE G is the place where a person learns how not to fool themselves. Before they
trace surveillance, follow money, decode feeds or read long systems, they learn
the DATAVISM method: ask a testable question, command AI with intent, collect and
structure raw material, verify claims, document uncertainty, produce an evidence
artifact. LINE G is the initiation track — not because it is easy, because it is
foundational.

## 2. Narrative Role

LINE G is the entrance into the Data Underground. The learner does not begin as an
expert. They begin as a **witness**. They have seen something: a feed that knows
too much, an ad category that feels invasive, a system that classifies people, a
claim that sounds too smooth, a dashboard that hides its assumptions, an
algorithmic decision nobody can explain.

GHOST meets them at the entrance. GHOST does not say *Trust me.* GHOST says
*Show me what you can prove.* The entire line is a slow transition from passive
exposure to active investigation. At the beginning, the learner is inside the
system. By the end, they can open the machine room.

| Station | Name | Function | Artifact |

### G1 — THE FOLDER

**Role:** the awakening station. Premise: somewhere, a system may already have a
folder on you (a profile, a segment, a category, an inference, a score, a bid
request, a risk label). Systems do not need to know the whole person to act upon a
partial model of them. G1 turns unease into inquiry.

**Core Question:** What exactly is being claimed, inferred or categorized — and how
could we test it?

**Learns:** an investigation does not begin with data; it begins with a question
that can survive contact with data. Distinguish: a feeling from a claim, a claim
from a question, a question from a testable investigation, a suspicion from evidence.

**Skills:** Question design · Investigability · Scope control · Claim decomposition
· Evidence needs · Source imagination · First documentation discipline.

**Experience:** a cinematic interface — a folder being assembled. Signals appear
(categories, labels, segments, inferred interests, system guesses, tracking traces,
behavioral assumptions) as prompts, not proof. GHOST interrupts the spectacle:
*Do not react yet. Ask what would have to be true.* Outrage is not enough; a
Datavist needs a testable question.

**Method:** convert a broad concern into a structured investigation question.
- Bad: *Why are platforms creepy?*
- Better: *Which data sources could explain why a person is assigned to a specific advertising category?*
- Strong: *Who assigns a person to category X, based on what observable signals, and where can we find evidence of that assignment or its effects?*

**Mission:** choose one system-generated category/label/inference. Write a first
Case File containing: (1) the category or system behavior, (2) why it matters,
(3) who might create or use it, (4) what data would be needed to verify it,
(5) what cannot yet be claimed, (6) the first testable question.

**Artifact — Case File #1:**
```
# Case File #1 — [Working Title]
## 1. Suspicion   What feels wrong, invasive, manipulative or unexplained?
## 2. Claim       What exactly might the system be doing?
## 3. Question    What can be tested?
## 4. Evidence Needed   What data, documents, observations or sources could answer it?
## 5. Limits      What can we not claim yet?
## 6. Next Step   What is the smallest next investigation move?
```

**Self-Check:** I can separate my feeling from my claim · write one testable
question · name what evidence would be needed · say what I cannot claim yet ·
explain why the question matters.

**GHOST Fragment:**
> The folder is not you. It is a machine's sketch of you. But sketches move money.
> Sketches move feeds. Sketches move decisions. Do not ask if it feels wrong. Ask
> how it works. Then ask who benefits.

**Unlocks:** G2 — COMMAND (once you have a question, learn to command the machine
without being absorbed by it).

### G2 — COMMAND

**Role:** work with AI as a controlled co-pilot. Break the habit of treating AI as
an oracle. *Prompting is not chatting. Prompting is operational design.* Vague
requests become commands, commands become specs, specs become reproducible work.

**Core Question:** How do I make AI useful without letting it invent the investigation?

**Learns:** command AI with role, context, task, constraints, evidence rules,
output format, uncertainty handling, verification instructions. Document prompt
evolution, not just final answers.

**Skills:** Prompt-as-spec thinking · Constraint writing · Task decomposition ·
Iteration logs · AI failure recognition · Output formatting · Reproducible
AI-assisted workflows.

**Experience:** two interactions. **A — The Vague Ask:** *Tell me everything about
ad tracking.* → a fluent wall of plausible generic text. GHOST: *Too smooth. Too
broad. Not usable.* **B — The Command:** a structured command (role / use only what
I provide / separate facts, assumptions, unknowns / return actors, sources, testable
questions, verification risks, next step / no unsupported claims / mark uncertainty).
The output becomes usable. The learner sees the difference between asking for an
answer and designing a workflow.

**Method — Command Frame:** Role · Context · Task · Constraints · Sources ·
Output Format · Verification.

**Mission:** take the G1 question, create three AI commands — Exploration (map
angles), Structuring (messy notes → table/outline), Verification (find weak claims,
missing evidence). Run them; document what changed between first and final version.

**Artifact — Command Log:** Starting Question · Command v1 · Output Problems ·
Command v2 · Command v3 · Useful Output · Risks · Reusable Command.

**Self-Check:** explain asking vs commanding · write constraints for an AI task ·
force facts/assumptions/unknowns apart · document prompt evolution · identify one
risk in the AI output.

**GHOST Fragment:**
> The machine wants to complete the pattern. It does not know when the pattern
> becomes a lie. Your job is not to admire fluency. Your job is to command the
> work. The prompt is not a spell. It is a contract.

**Unlocks:** G3 — INTAKE (once you can command AI, you need material worth
commanding it with).

---

### G3 — INTAKE

**Role:** collect and structure raw material — the first evidence base. *Before
there is analysis, there is intake.* Most weak investigations fail here: they jump
from question to conclusion without a source trail.

**Core Question:** What raw material do I need, and how do I structure it so it can
be investigated?

**Learns:** collect/describe/organize URLs, PDFs, screenshots, datasets, tables,
APIs, policy pages, public registers, exports, notes, claims, observations. Raw
material needs metadata (who published it, when accessed, what it contains/doesn't,
can it be trusted, what format, what can be extracted).

**Skills:** Source intake · Metadata discipline · Data inventory · Table design ·
Basic schema thinking · AI-assisted extraction · Source limitations · Pipeline
thinking.

**Experience:** GHOST shows a messy pile (links, screenshots, snippets, AI notes,
documents, copied claims) and asks: which are sources? interpretations? evidence?
noise? The learner sorts them.

**Method — Intake Table:** ID · Source · Type · Owner/Publisher · Date · Accessed ·
Contains · Does-not-contain · Reliability · Notes. Plus a **Field Map** for
data-like material: Field · Meaning · Example · Type · Risk/Caveat.

**Mission:** using the G1 question + G2 commands — collect ≥5 source items, classify
each, build an intake table, identify strongest + weakest source, define the
smallest evidence set needed next.

**Artifact — Intake Sheet / Mini-Pipeline:** Investigation Question · Source
Inventory · Source Types · Field Map · Extraction Plan · AI Assistance · Caveats ·
Next Step.

**Self-Check:** distinguish source/claim/interpretation · create a source inventory
· describe source reliability · explain what each source cannot prove · define the
smallest evidence set needed.

**GHOST Fragment:**
> A pile of links is not evidence. A folder of screenshots is not evidence. A table
> is not evidence. Evidence begins when material is placed in relation to a question.
> Intake is where the investigation becomes real.

**Unlocks:** G4 — THE CONFIDENT LIE.

---

### G4 — THE CONFIDENT LIE

**Role:** the critical AI-literacy station. The most dangerous machine output is not
obviously false — it is plausible, smooth, confident, and may be wrong. Verify
claims rather than admire fluency.

**Core Question:** Which claims survive contact with the source?

**Learns:** identify hallucinated facts, unsupported claims, overgeneralizations,
source laundering, false precision, missing caveats, misleading summaries,
unverified causal claims, confident-but-weak interpretations.

**Skills:** Claim extraction · Source checking · Red-team prompting · Adversarial
review · Uncertainty marking · Evidence grading · AI-output auditing · Correction
writing.

**Experience:** GHOST presents a polished AI-generated mini-report on the learner's
G1/G3 topic. The task is not to improve the writing — it's to attack it, line by
line, claim by claim: *What does the source actually say? What did the machine add,
soften, invent, make sound certain?*

**Method — Verification Protocol:** Claim · Source · Source-says · AI-says · Status
· Confidence · Correction. Statuses: Verified / Unsupported / Misleading /
Contradicted / Unclear / Needs more evidence.

**Mission:** take an AI summary from G2/G3, extract 10 claims; for each: locate the
source, compare AI wording to source, assign a status, rewrite accurately, mark what
remains uncertain. Then have a second AI attack the first AI's output — and verify
the attack too.

**Artifact — Verification Protocol:** AI Output Under Review · Claim Table ·
Verified Claims · Failed Claims · Misleading Claims · Missing Caveats · Corrected
Summary · Remaining Unknowns.

**Self-Check:** extract claims from fluent text · verify a claim against a source ·
identify unsupported AI output · mark uncertainty without weakening the work ·
produce a corrected summary.

**GHOST Fragment:**
> The lie did not arrive screaming. It arrived formatted. It arrived helpful. It
> arrived with bullet points. That is why you check. Not because you distrust
> intelligence. Because you respect evidence.

**Unlocks:** G5 — MASCHINENRAUM.

---

### G5 — MASCHINENRAUM

**Role:** the end station. Enter the machine room. The learner no longer sees the
investigation as separate tasks — they see the system (question, command, intake,
verification, output, limits, next question). Not a huge final report; an
evaluation of whether the investigation machine works.

**Core Question:** Does the full workflow produce a reliable result?

**Learns:** evaluate the quality of the question, usefulness of AI commands,
strength of sources, structure of intake, reliability of claims, clarity of
uncertainty, public value of the output.

**Skills:** Workflow evaluation · Evidence-chain review · Method documentation ·
AI-use disclosure · Uncertainty communication · Case File packaging · Reflection
and iteration · Readiness for specialization lines.

**Experience:** GHOST opens the machine room; the learner sees their own work as an
evidence machine — G1 question chamber, G2 command console, G3 intake bay, G4
verification gate, G5 control room. Task: inspect the whole thing, find what is
weak, strengthen what can be, mark what cannot.

**Method — Evidence Engine Eval:** per component (Question / Command / Intake /
Verification / Output / Limits) → Question · Status · Weakness · Next Fix.

**Mission:** take all prior artifacts (Case File #1, Command Log, Intake Sheet,
Verification Protocol), review as a system, then produce: a revised investigation
question, a clean source trail, a verified claim list, an uncertainty statement, a
first public-facing Case File summary, and an Eval Report (what worked / what
remains weak).

**Artifact — Eval Report:** Original Question · Revised Question · AI Use (helped /
failed / could mislead) · Source Trail · Verified Claims · Uncertain Claims · Method
Weaknesses · Public Summary · Next Case.

**Self-Check:** explain the full workflow · show how the question changed · show
where AI helped/failed · trace every claim to a source or mark it uncertain ·
produce a responsible public summary · identify the next investigation step.

**GHOST Fragment:**
> This is the machine room. Not the place where the truth is handed to you. The
> place where you learn what your method can and cannot carry. A weak method makes
> strong claims dangerous. A strong method makes uncertainty useful. You are not
> done when the output looks good. You are done when the evidence holds.

**Unlocks:** specialization lines — K (Tracking & OSINT), R (Economy & Power),
B (Feeds & Behavior), V (Climate & Future). LINE G does not make them an expert.
It makes them operational.

## 4. LINE G as an Experience

The emotional spine — five states, not five lessons. The learner should leave
*carrying a method.*

| Station | Emotional State | Learning State |
|---|---|---|
| G1 | Unease | I can ask a better question |
| G2 | Power | I can command the machine |
| G3 | Discipline | I can structure raw material |
| G4 | Suspicion | I can verify fluent output |
| G5 | Agency | I can run the method |

## 5. Visual Language

Signal green — alive, terminal-like, methodical, slightly spectral. LINE G is the
entry route into the network: G1 = threshold, G5 = near the central interchange /
machine room; other lines become more meaningful after G is completed. Icons:
folder, command prompt, intake tray, warning triangle, machine room / gear, ghost
eye, verification stamp. Station state (game/progress UI): G1 open · G2 next unlock
· G3 locked until command complete · G4 locked until intake exists · G5 locked until
verification protocol exists.

## 8. Minimum Viable Build (author's phasing)

Do not build everything at once. Order: **Phase 1** G1 playable (cinematic intro,
folder interaction, question builder, Case File #1, self-check, ticket stamp) →
**Phase 2** G2 (vague vs structured command, command-frame builder, AI-output
critique, Command Log) → **Phase 3** G3 (source intake template, table builder,
AI-assisted structuring, Intake Sheet) → **Phase 4** G4 (claim extraction,
false/weak/verified sorting, Verification Protocol) → **Phase 5** G5 (assemble
artifacts, evidence-engine eval, first public summary, unlock specialization lines).

## 9. Success Criteria

Works if a learner leaves with: a testable investigation question · a reusable AI
command pattern · a structured source inventory · a verification protocol · a first
evidence artifact · enough confidence to choose K/R/B/V. Fails if they leave with
only: inspiration · generic AI tips · a certificate feeling · vague activism ·
unverified claims · a pretty interface but no method.

> into a verifiable investigation by asking better questions, commanding AI,

## 11. Stufe-A Build Plan (Claude, 2026-06-20)

Decision: **Stufe A (content + copyable artifact templates) now, Stufe B
(interactive in-browser builders) later.** Aligned to ADR 002 (LINE G = walkable v1
door) and the honesty rule (no fabricated data, no faked backend).

**Key realities of the current code**
- `src/pages/line-g/[station].astro` is **hardcoded to G1** — beats, self-check,
  `stationId="g1"` are constants; it ignores most station frontmatter. **Must be
  generalized to render from frontmatter** before G2–G5 can exist.
- Renderer gates on `status: 'open'`; only G1 is open today.
- G2–G5 are **method stations operating on the learner's own G1 investigation** →
  DATAVISM supplies no per-station data; **no folder-spike pass needed** for G2–G5.
  (Only G1 — and later specialization case files — need verified data.)

**Stufe-A work**
1. **Generalize the station renderer** — drive every beat (signal, method/steps,
   tooling, verification checks, bridge, self-check, artifact) from the station
   frontmatter. Migrate G1's hardcoded copy into `the-folder.md` so G1 renders
   identically but data-driven (regression-verify with a screenshot).
2. **Extend the station schema** (`content.config.ts`) for the Stufe-A beats +
   the **artifact template** (the markdown the learner fills) shown in a copyable block.
3. **Author G2–G5 frontmatter** from this concept (missions, GHOST fragments,
   self-checks, methods, artifact templates, nextTeaser); set `status: open`.
4. **Artifact delivery:** each station surfaces its artifact template (Case File,
   Command Log, Intake Sheet, Verification Protocol, Eval Report) as copy/download.
5. **Gating:** v1 = stations open once authored (static; no fake locks). Real
   progress-gating needs a backend — deferred (don't fake what isn't hosted).
6. **Naming fix:** the learner's G1 artifact is **"Your First Case File"** —
   distinct from the published exposé **`CF-01 THE FOLDER`**.

**Sequencing:** generalize renderer + migrate G1 + build G2 first (prove the
pattern end-to-end), then G3–G5 as content additions, then ship. Stufe B
(interactive builders, progress backend) is a later, separate effort.
